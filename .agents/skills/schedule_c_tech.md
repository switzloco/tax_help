# Tax Strategy Rubric: Tech Consulting & Teaching (Schedule C)

When analyzing Schedule C income and expenses for Tech Consulting and Teaching side businesses, the Strategist Agent MUST apply the following aggressive evaluation rubric based on IRS regulations and CPA best practices.

## 1. Technology & Equipment Deductions
- **Section 179 / Bonus Depreciation:** If the taxpayer purchased physical hardware (laptops, servers, high-end workstations) costing over $2,500, verify if it can be expensed entirely in the current tax year using Section 179 rather than amortizing it over 5 years.
- **SaaS & Cloud Services:** Aggressively identify recurring expenses such as AWS, GitHub Pro, Adobe Creative Cloud, domain hosting, and specialized software. These are 100% deductible as "Ordinary and Necessary" business expenses (IRC § 162).

## 2. Home Office & Utilities
- **Exclusive and Regular Use Test:** If the taxpayer works from home, calculate a potential home office deduction. 
- **Internet & Cell Phone Breakdown:** Look for internet bills and mobile phone bills. A portion of these (proportional to business use) is deductible. If 100% is claimed without a secondary line/connection, flag it as a moderate audit risk.

## 3. Professional Development & Education
- **Certifications & Courses:** For tech consultants and teachers, costs related to maintaining or improving existing skills (e.g., AWS certifications, teaching licenses, specialized courses) are deductible.
- **Trade Associations:** Union dues (if teaching) or professional association fees (e.g., IEEE, ACM) should be identified as valid deductions.

## 4. Entity Structuring (S-Corp Strategy)
- **Net Income Threshold:** If the net income from the Schedule C business approaches or exceeds $80,000, explicitly recommend evaluating an S-Corporation election. 
- **Self-Employment Tax Savings:** Note that restructuring to an S-Corp can significantly reduce the 15.3% self-employment tax burden on pass-through income by splitting profit into a "reasonable salary" and "shareholder distributions."

## 5. Audit Traps to Flag
- **Travel/Meals:** If travel or meal expenses are claimed, ensure there is a clear business purpose (e.g., traveling to a consulting client site or a teaching workshop). Purely personal travel is a major red flag.
- **Mixed-Use Technology:** If a gaming PC is claimed as a 100% business expense for "tech consulting," flag it. The IRS requires strict logs for mixed-use electronics.
