# Agent Skill: IRS 2025 Federal & State Tax Brackets

This skill defines the official IRS tax brackets, standard deductions, and state tax parameters for the 2025 tax year. The Audit Agent should use these figures to perform precise mathematical calculations.

## 1. 2025 Federal Standard Deduction
- **Married Filing Jointly (MFJ):** **$31,500**
- Single / Married Filing Separately (MFS): $15,750
- Head of Household (HoH): $23,600

---

## 2. 2025 Federal Income Tax Brackets (Married Filing Jointly)
For MFJ taxpayers in the 2025 tax year, the tax brackets are structured as follows:

| Tax Rate | Taxable Income Bracket |
| :--- | :--- |
| **10%** | $0 to $23,850 |
| **12%** | $23,851 to $96,950 |
| **22%** | $96,951 to $206,700 |
| **24%** | $206,701 to $394,600 |
| **32%** | $394,601 to $501,050 |
| **35%** | $501,051 to $751,600 |
| **37%** | $751,601 or more |

---

## 3. Michigan State Tax Parameters (2025)
- **Flat Tax Rate:** **4.25%** of Michigan taxable income.
- **Personal Exemption:** **$5,800** per taxpayer and dependent.
- **State Taxable Income:** Calculated starting from federal Adjusted Gross Income (AGI) with state-specific additions and subtractions (such as the personal exemption).

---

## 4. Reference Formula for Combined Tax Bracket (Planning)
For high-income taxpayers in the 32% federal bracket:
- **Federal Bracket:** 32%
- **Michigan State Bracket:** 4.25%
- **Combined Marginal Rate:** **36.25%**
- **Tax Optimization Savings Formula:**
  $$\text{Tax Savings} = \text{Deductible Loss/Expense} \times \text{Combined Marginal Rate (36.25\%)}$$
