# Agent Skill: Extension Filing & Penalty Rules

This skill details post-extension filing deadlines, late payment and late filing penalties, safe harbor rules for high-income taxpayers, and interest calculations.

## 1. Deadlines & Extensions (Form 4868)

- **Original Due Date:** April 15.
- **Extended Due Date:** **October 15** of the filing year (via Form 4868).
- **CRITICAL DISTINCTION:** An extension of time to file is **NOT** an extension of time to pay. Tax liabilities must be paid by April 15 to avoid interest and late payment penalties.

---

## 2. Penalty & Interest Calculations

### Failure-to-Pay Penalty (IRC § 6651(a)(2))
- **Rate:** **0.5% per month** (or fraction of a month) on the unpaid tax balance.
- **Maximum:** **25%** of the unpaid tax.
- **Grace Period:** If a taxpayer files Form 4868 and pays at least 90% of their actual tax liability by the original due date, the late payment penalty is waived, provided the remaining balance is paid by the October 15 extended deadline.

### Failure-to-File Penalty (IRC § 6651(a)(1))
- **Rate:** **5% per month** (or fraction of a month) on the unpaid tax balance for returns filed after the deadline (April 15, or October 15 if an extension was filed).
- **Maximum:** **25%** of the unpaid tax.
- **Combined Penalty Adjustment:** If both the failure-to-file and failure-to-pay penalties apply in the same month, the failure-to-file penalty (5%) is reduced by the failure-to-pay penalty (0.5%), resulting in a combined rate of **5% per month** (4.5% late filing + 0.5% late payment).
- **60-Day Minimum Penalty:** If a return is over 60 days late, the minimum failure-to-file penalty is the lesser of the statutory minimum (e.g., $485 for tax year 2024/2025 returns filed in 2025/2026) or 100% of the unpaid tax.

### Interest (IRC § 6621)
- Interest compounds daily on any unpaid tax and penalties from the original due date (April 15) until the date of full payment.
- The interest rate is determined quarterly and is equal to the federal short-term rate plus **3 percentage points**.

## 3. Safe Harbor Rules for High-Income Taxpayers (IRC § 6654)

To avoid the estimated tax underpayment penalty (Form 2210), taxpayers must make timely quarterly payments equal to or exceeding the safe harbor thresholds.

### Safe Harbor Thresholds
1. **90% Rule:** Pay at least **90%** of the tax liability shown on the current year's tax return.
2. **100% Rule (Standard):** Pay **100%** of the tax liability shown on the prior year's tax return.
3. **110% Rule (High-Income):** If the taxpayer's Adjusted Gross Income (AGI) on the prior year's return exceeded **$150,000** (for Married Filing Jointly [MFJ] or Single):
   - **Requirement:** Must pay **110%** of the prior year's tax liability.
   - **Case Profile Evaluation:** With a W-2 income of **$480,000**, this household is classified as high-income. They must meet the **110% safe harbor** to avoid underpayment penalties if they rely on the prior year's tax liability.
